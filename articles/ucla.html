On April 25, 2024, a pro-Palestinian encampment accumulatd on the UCLA campus. Amid rising tensions, violence eventually broke out on April 30, 2024. The conflict at UCLA began with a pro-Palestinian encampment established to protest the university's investments in Israel and to raise awareness about the Palestinian cause. This encampment attracted significant attention and eventually led to confrontations with pro-Israel activists who counter-protested near the site.

Purpose of Encampment: The pro-Palestinian encampment was set up to protest UCLA's investments in Israel and to draw attention to the Palestinian cause. The encampment aimed to highlight issues faced by Palestinians and call for divestment from companies supporting Israeli policies.

Violence and Confrontations: The situation escalated when pro-Israel counter-protesters attacked the encampment. The attackers used pepper spray, sticks, stones, and fireworks, leading to violent clashes between the two groups. This violence prompted a response from the university and police.

University Response: UCLA declared the encampment illegal and threatened students with disciplinary consequences such as expulsion or suspension. Following the violence, the university ordered the encampment to disperse. The police used stun grenades and tear gas to disperse the protesters, resulting in multiple arrests and injuries.

Police Involvement: The UCLA Police Department (UCPD) and the Los Angeles Police Department (LAPD) were involved in managing the situation. The LAPD was called to support after the violence escalated, and officers used force to disperse the protesters and counter-protesters.

Opinion: Pro-Palestine

  • A core Pro-Palestine belief is that the protests must not stop until the war ends. This is rooted in a belief that Israel's response to Oct 7 is not proportional and is not last resort.
  • The core belief of protest organizers is to be peaceful. Violent protesters have been condemned. Often, most violent protesters are not UCLA students but rather outside agitators.
  • Pro-Palestine protesters discuss police brutality and unjust violence on protesters. Medics have treated broken bones caused by police weapons. For peaceful protesters, this treatment is seen as a violation of the right to assembly.[1]

Opinion: Pro-Israel

  • Pro-Israel students on campus have expressed feeling unsafe and marginalized due to the protests, often consider the protests to be anti-semitic.
  • The violence inflicted by counterprotesters is often blamed on pro-israeli students and faculty. Many Jewish students condemned the violence and emphasized that it was not representative of the broader Jewish community at UCLA. Additionally, they pointed out that many of the violent protesters were not affiliated with the university, complicating the situation further.
  • While most pro-palestine protesters simply demand an end to the war, there is significant rhetoric implying that Jews should completely evacuate the Middle East. Pro-Israel opinions point to phrases and slogans such as "from the river to the sea", and "anti-Zionist", where Zionism is the political belief that Jews should be allowed in Israel.
